5 things you should do every day

6 July 2015 | Tweet Comment | Motivation, Lifestyle

Alright, I'm going to apologize in advance. You’ve probably come across many variations of different tips and tricks to survive your day.. Well, survive might be too strong of a word, so let’s say to help you with your daily routine. I always catch myself in the middle of the afternoon either remembering that I forgot to eat breakfast, or that something I did differently caused me stress. Most likely because I was working — well, let’s get real:

I was probably napping.

I'll be honest, I don't do these all every day, or even most of the week, but I hope they might make your day enormously better:

1. Eat Breakfast


I know. You’ve heard this a million times. "Breakfast is the most important meal of the day.” Well, I am here to tell you again! If you know you got something big going on later in your day, you might just need that energy boost or need to stay focused throughout the day. Even a banana or two can give you that boost needed. Don't let your day suffer because you forgot to eat or didn't feel like it.

Take 5 minutes out of your day to leave out some food for you the next day. Future you will thank you, even when they are in a rush.

Don’t end up like Terry Crews. I mean, just don’t let your body go without food.

2. Exercise


Is it really that hard? It's not one of my favourite things, but it's got to be done. Majority of the time we assume we're in good shape because we're not showing any drastic negative signs of bad health. We could all take a page from Barney Stinson, you just have to make the decision to step out the door. The rest of it is a breeze.

You don't have to go to the gym an excessive amount, all you need is 30 minutes / 3 days a week to stay in good physical health … Or 6 days, 15 minutes each, and so on.. I think that math works.

3. Plan Your Day


Three Words: Prioritize, prioritize, and prioritize. It’s so simple to lose focus throughout the day. Before you know it, your day will fly by if you don’t take control. If you work best at a certain time of the day, make sure your most important work gets scheduled then. Oh, and take lots of breaks — my personal favourite is 52 minutes working, 17 minute break, repeat.

4. Don't Check Your Social Media Right Away


This is a tough one. As we move farther and farther into the tech abyss, it is almost impossible not to check your social feeds, your e-mails, or messages when you wake up.

I do it all the time, and I’m sure a lot of others do as well — it has to stop. It’s a large distraction from your daily planning.

A good tip is to wait until you’re out of the house, or if you are a stay-at-home type, at least wait until 10am before you give that screen a look.

5. Single Task


Goes without saying.

Stay focused — Don’t let that evil multi-taking into your life. Stick to single task to stay productive and efficient.

6. BONUS


Something that I rarely do is say the word,"No". I have always been someone who wants to be able to take on as many things as possible, and it doesn’t always work out they way I had hope. Sometimes, but not always.

Not every decision you say yes to will benefit you, and the same goes for the opposite. Saying yes or no all the time is not the answer, it's all about balance (don't get too involved with FOMO -- Fear Of Missing Out). it is important to value your time by doing what's important to you.

I have only scratched the surface on what you could be doing to improve your daily life, but that's for another day. Thanks for reading, and I hope this post will possitively impact you and your quality of life!
