Jamaican Adventures - Part I

21 July 2015 | Tweet Comment | Travel, Lifestyle

It’s been almost three weeks being in Jamaica, but I’ll be starting to post every so often on my adventures in Jamaica over the next several months. It’s been quite the rush so far and I’m excited about the rest of my time here.

A little back-story:

In late March of this year I had participated in an event that involved many Computing Science and Business students, faculty, and industry leaders from around the Halifax area, and shortly following the event I, along with another student, were approached about the opportunity to study in Jamaica as part of the Queen Elizabeth Scholars program. The idea of the program is that students from all over Canada get the opportunity to study in Jamaica, while many students from Jamaica get to study in Canada.

I thought it would be great to study in another country as an international student.

So I got time off work.

I booked my flights.

I said: ‘Let’s do it’.

The first thing I saw when I arrived in Kingston, Jamaica is the photo at the top of this post. It was welcoming, but it really set the bar high for me. Almost as if it finally hit me that I was I was finally in Jamaica after the long paperwork process.

After getting through the airport - bag check, customs, and a long wait at immigration - I was ready to go and hit the road.

My first day was relatively fast paced, quickly dropping off my luggage into my apartment and going straight to the University of Technology to meet some of the people that I would be working with.

I was lucky to have arrived the day before Jamaica’s ‘Independence Day’ because of all the great festivities I got to see at their Grand Gala. It had not been planned, but it was a good experience seeing how another country celebrated it’s country’s independence.

The third day I was in Jamaica I had gotten all settled in, and it was my first day of work. Most of the day had been orientation, but it was good to get-to-know some co-workers in my department and also get to know about the company I would be working on (more on this later).

Early last week I had completed all of my work permit and immigration documents and was excited. One thing I didn’t plan for was the length of the process, and the costs associated with it. Something I would do differently next time is set up a budget for the first few expenses when traveling to cover the travel cost, first month’s rent, and application costs, which added up quickly.

I’m excited for what the next few weeks will bring me. Places i am looking to go visit are the Bob Marley Museum, hike Blue Mountain, Tracks And Records (Usain Bolt’s restaurant), and Emancipation Park. That’s not all, but I’ll get to each one on the list one step at a time.
